Problem: Raw scores cluster around 50%. Two people at 48% and 52% look "very similar" even though one leans each way with little conviction. The distance metric needs to account for this.
Step 1 — Power-curve stretching:
Each score is transformed: adjusted = 50 + sign(d) × |d/50|^0.6 × 50 where d = raw − 50.

The exponent 0.6 means small deviations near centre get amplified more than large ones. A 52% (barely leaning) stretches to ~58%. A 75% (strong conviction) stretches to ~83%. The further you already are from centre, the less additional stretching — you're already expressing a clear position.
Step 2 — Similarity metric:
similarity = 100 − mean(|adj_a − adj_b|) across all 7 dimensions.

Same formula as before, but the stretched inputs mean genuine differences produce larger gaps and centre-hugging "agreement" gets correctly downweighted.
